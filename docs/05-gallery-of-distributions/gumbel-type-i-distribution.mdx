# Gumbel (Type I) Distribution

## Where do you meet this distribution?

- Extreme value theory (EVT)
- [Risk management -- Operational risk](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operational_risk)

## Shape of Distribution

### Basic Properties

- Two parameters $\alpha, \beta$ are required ([How can you get these](https://www.ntrand.com/ntgumbelparam/)).

  $$
  \beta>0
  $$

- Continuous distribution defined on entire range
- This distribution is always asymmetric.

### Probability

- [Cumulative distribution function](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_cumulative)

  $$
  F(x)=\exp\left[-\exp\left(-\frac{x-\alpha}{\beta}\right)\right]
  $$

- [Probability density function](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_probability)

  $$
  f(x)=\frac{1}{\beta}\exp\left(-\frac{x-\alpha}{\beta}\right)\exp\left[-\exp\left(-\frac{x-\alpha}{\beta}\right)\right]
  $$

### Quantile

- Inverse function of [cumulative distribution function](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_cumulative)

  $$
  F^{-1}(P)=\alpha-\beta\ln\ln\frac{1}{P}
  $$

## Characteristics

### Mean -- Where is the "center" of the distribution? ([Definition](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_mean))

- [Mean](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_mean) of the distribution is given as

  $$
  \alpha+\gamma\beta
  $$

  where $\gamma$ is [Euler's constant](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_EulerConstant).

### Standard Deviation -- How wide does the distribution spread? ([Definition](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_standard_deviation))

- [Variance](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_variance) of the distribution is given as

  $$
  \beta^2\zeta(2)
  $$

  where $\zeta(\cdot)$ is [Riemann zeta function](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_Riemann).

  [Standard Deviation](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_standard_deviation) is a positive square root of [Variance](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_variance).

### Skewness -- Which side is the distribution distorted into? ([Definition](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_skewness))

- [Skewness](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_skew) of the distribution is given as

  $$
  -\frac{12\sqrt{6}\zeta(3)}{\pi^3}=-1.139547099\cdots
  $$

  where $\zeta(\cdot)$ is [Riemann zeta function](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_Riemann).

### Kurtosis -- Sharp or Dull, consequently Fat Tail or Thin Tail ([Definition](https://www.ntrand.com/glossary/#local_kurtosis))

- Kurtosis of the distribution is $2.4$

## Random Numbers

- Random number x is generated by inverse function method, which is for uniform random U,

  $$
  x=\alpha-\beta\ln\ln\frac{1}{U}
  $$

## NtRand Functions

- If you already have parameters of the distribution
  - Generating random numbers based on Mersenne Twister algorithm: [NTRANDGUMBEL](https://www.ntrand.com/ntrandgumbel/)
  - Computing probability : [NTGUMBELDIST](https://www.ntrand.com/ntgumbeldist/)
  - Computing mean : [NTGUMBELMEAN](https://www.ntrand.com/ntgumbelmean/)
  - Computing standard deviation : [NTGUMBELSTDEV](https://www.ntrand.com/ntgumbelskew/)
  - Computing skewness : [NTGUMBELSKEW](https://www.ntrand.com/ntgumbelskew/)
  - Computing kurtosis : [NTGUMBELKURT](https://www.ntrand.com/ntgumbelkurt/)
  - Computing moments above at once : [NTGUMBELMOM](https://www.ntrand.com/ntgumbelmom/)
- If you know mean and standard deviation of the distribution
  - Estimating parameters of the distribution:[NTGUMBELRPARAM](https://www.ntrand.com/ntgumbelparam/)
